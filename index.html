~60 Min ¬∑ Script ¬∑ Observation ¬∑ Tech Issues ¬∑ Documentation
üìù Session Opening Script
How to Use This Script Lead RA (setup RA) stands at front, assistant RA at back. Both introduce themselves. Read the script below to participants. After the announcement, the person in back should come to the front of the room. It is important that participants feel they have privacy ‚Äî when you come to their station, be as unobtrusive as possible.
Lead RA

Thank you for participating in the marketing research studies. My name is [NAME of the person making the announcement].

Assistant RA

Hi, and I am [name of the person in the back of the room].

Lead RA

We will be taking you through a series of studies today.

Over the course of this session, you will be participating in several studies related to marketing and consumer behavior.

Lead RA ‚Äî Pacing

Everyone will be here for the ENTIRE session, so please take your time with the studies. There's no sense rushing. Everybody has their own pace, and you will not be penalized if you do not finish all studies.

Lead RA ‚Äî ID Instructions

You have a 6-digit ID written on the Post-it I handed you when you walked in. Each study will prompt you to write down or enter this number. Make sure to use THIS number and NOT your USC ID‚Äîwe need the 6-digit number to give you credit for taking all the studies today.

Lead RA ‚Äî Tech

If you experience any technical difficulties with a study, please raise your hand and let one of us know.

Lead RA ‚Äî Headphones (if applicable)

[If one of the studies requires headphones,] One of the studies requires using the headphones. We will let you know when to put on the headphones.

Lead RA ‚Äî Study Order

There is a SET ORDER in which these studies are arranged. You will NOT be taking the studies in the order they are listed in the folder in front of you. The reason for that order is that it allows me to keep track that you were here and get credit.

We will all start the first study together, and then EVERY TIME you finish a study, raise your hand, and one of us will come get you started on the next study.

Lead RA ‚Äî Focus

It is really important that you do not engage in any tasks other than these studies during this session.

Lead RA ‚Äî Questions

Are there any questions before we get started?

[Pause for questions. Address any concerns.]

Lead RA ‚Äî Start

Ok, your first study is [NAME OF STUDY 1]. Please remember to raise your hand when you are done.

‚úî Session Start Checklist
  • All participants have their 6-digit ID on Post-it
  • Headphones distributed (if applicable)
  • Participants ready to begin
  • Both RAs positioned and ready
üëÅ Observation & Monitoring

Privacy is critical. Be discreet when monitoring.

Being Discreet Record IDs subtly using a small notepad or Google Spreadsheet on the Proctor/RA Desk. Don't stare ‚Äî use casual glances while walking around. Position yourself to see multiple stations without being intrusive. If a participant notices you, smile briefly and look away naturally. Try to gauge if people are still working but don't rush them.

Watch for: Rushing, confusion, distress, pattern-clicking, excessive dictionary use, not paying attention.

üìä Session Documentation
‚¨õ Documentation Is Mandatory Document EVERYTHING in Google Spreadsheet: tech issues, behavior, departures, disruptions. IDs only ‚Äî NEVER names. Be specific & objective. When in doubt, document it.

Failing to document issues can compromise research validity and waste months of work. PhD Supervisors rely on your observations to make informed decisions about data inclusion.
Accessing the Google Spreadsheet The PhD Supervisor shares the spreadsheet URL each week. Access it on the Proctor/RA Desk computer during your shift and keep it open throughout the session for real-time documentation. If you don't have the URL, contact the PhD Supervisor immediately.

Best Practices Be specific ("Completed Study A in 2 min, typical is 8‚Äì10 min" not just "rushed"). Note patterns across participants. Document in real time when possible; be discreet. All documentation must be entered before you sign out ‚Äî memories fade quickly. When you observe something unusual, ask: "Could this affect how this participant responded?" If yes, document it.

Unsure whether to document? Document it anyway and mention it to the PhD Supervisor. It's always better to over-document than to lose important context about potential data issues.
